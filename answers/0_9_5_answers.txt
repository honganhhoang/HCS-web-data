6.
To register, contact your CAL disability advisor and express interest in participating in the group. Be specific about which group you want to join. Your advisor will forward you an email referral with instructions on booking an intake appointment with the group facilitator. Attend a 30-minute group screening meeting with the facilitator to determine if the group is a good fit. Commit to attending all 8 sessions. Consider your mental and emotional energy for consistent participation. Contact Sean for further questions or individual counseling.

7.
- Students must commit to attending all 8 sessions
- Missed sessions can be discussed during intake with the CAL counsellor
- Consider if you have the mental and emotional energy to participate consistently
- Exam stress is not considered an emergency circumstance
- New members will not be admitted once groups begin to foster safety and consistency for existing members

8.
Graduate students can determine if they have the mental and emotional energy to participate in the group consistently by considering their ability to commit to attending all 8 sessions, assessing if they can handle stress during exam periods, and recognizing that missed sessions can only be discussed in emergencies or medical appointments.

9.
Sean Heaslip is the HCS counselor liaison with the Centre for Accessible Learning (CAL). He provides individual and group counseling services.

10.
Graduate students can contact Sean at sean_heaslip@sfu.ca for further questions about the group or individual counseling services.
