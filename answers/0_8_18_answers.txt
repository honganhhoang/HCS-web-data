1.
Healthy Campus Community is a foundational, award-winning initiative by SFU’s Health Promotion team that takes a systemic, institution-wide approach to enhance conditions conducive to well-being. It ensures that policies, processes, learning environments, physical spaces, and other aspects of the institutional environment are designed to enhance and support student mental health and well-being. The initiative directly contributes to promoting and protecting student mental health & well-being at SFU.

2.
The initiative aims to enhance student mental health and well-being by taking a systemic, institution-wide approach to create healthy settings that prioritize mental health and well-being. It ensures that policies, processes, learning environments, physical spaces, and other aspects of the institutional environment are designed to enhance and support student well-being. The initiative also contributes directly to What’s Next: the SFU Strategy by promoting and protecting student mental health and well-being.

3.
Core principles: Enhancing conditions conducive to well-being, promoting and protecting student mental health & well-being, contributing to the health and success of the institution and its community members.

Vision: Creating cultures, systems, curriculum, programs, and service enhancements that prioritize mental health and well-being for students within academic and non-academic environments.

- Pandemic fatigue is associated with anxiety, social isolation, financial stress, and increased academic load, all related to mental well-being.

15.
The rationale behind SFU's Healthy Campus Community Initiative and its key projects is to create a systemic, institution-wide approach to enhance conditions that are conducive to well-being for students, staff, and faculty. It is based on the World Health Organization (WHO) Healthy University Framework and aims to positively influence the health and well-being of campus members.
