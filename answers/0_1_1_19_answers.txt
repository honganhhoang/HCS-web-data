1.
The purpose of the Student Conduct Policy at Simon Fraser University is to define and provide examples of prohibited behavior considered to be misconduct, establish a process for responding to complaints about misconduct, and ensure a scholarly community characterized by honesty, diversity, free inquiry, and mutual respect.

2.
Vice-Provost and Associate Vice-President, Students and International (“VPSI”)

3.
Examples of Misconduct are provided in Appendix B to this policy.

4.
Complaints of alleged misconduct at the university are received and managed according to the Student Conduct Procedures.

5.
The Vice-Provost and Associate Vice-President, Students and International is responsible for implementing the policy and monitoring its operational aspects and procedures.

6.
Interim measures may be imposed while an allegation of misconduct is being addressed.

7.
The university ensures procedural fairness in addressing alleged misconduct by addressing misconduct in an objective, fair, and timely manner, having an impartial and appropriately qualified person manage and/or investigate complaints, allowing the respondent to be accompanied by a support person to meetings, providing information and guidance through the Ombudsperson, informing the respondent of the particulars of the allegation and giving them an opportunity to respond.

8.
Disciplinary measures may include but are not limited to: warning, reprimand, probation, restitution, educational programming, community service, temporary suspension, permanent suspension, and expulsion.

9.
The policy needs to be reviewed at least once every five years.

10.
The President has the authority to suspend a student according to the policy.
