Breathing retraining can help with anxiety by:
- Teaching individuals how to regulate their breathing patterns
- Helping to reduce symptoms of anxiety such as rapid heartbeat and shallow breathing
- Promoting relaxation and reducing feelings of tension and stress

The Vicious Cycle of Anxiety involves a pattern of anxious thoughts, physical symptoms, and avoidance behaviors that perpetuate feelings of anxiety and distress.

4.
Behavioural Experiments can help with anxiety by:
1. Challenging and testing unhelpful thoughts and beliefs
2. Providing evidence to support more balanced thinking
3. Encouraging individuals to try new behaviors and see the outcomes
4. Helping individuals learn new coping strategies and skills for managing anxiety

5.
Safety behaviors are actions or behaviors that individuals engage in to reduce anxiety or avoid feared situations. However, in the long run, safety behaviors can actually maintain or worsen anxiety by preventing individuals from learning that they can cope with their fears.

6.
Progressive Muscle Relaxation can help with anxiety by teaching individuals to relax their muscles and reduce physical tension, which can help alleviate feelings of anxiety and stress.
