Academic concessions are typically granted when unexpected situations or circumstances prevent students from participating in course-related activities, such as missing a class, completing graded work or exams.

2.
illness, accident, family situation, unanticipated changes in personal responsibilities

3.
Academic concessions are granted at the discretion of individual instructors, while academic accommodations are required modifications or additions to ensure fair access.

4.
Students should always speak to their instructor as a first step. 
Students are required to complete the Academic Concession Form when seeking Academic Concessions from their instructor. 
Students are not required to present medical documentation for minor or short-term illnesses. 
Instructors can request medical documentation for students missing a final examination or an assessment worth more than 20% of the final course grade. 
Instructors must make clear in the course syllabus instances when students must present medical documentation.

5.
An AE grade is granted for incomplete coursework on medical or compassionate grounds, while a DE grade is a temporary grade assigned at the end of the term for incomplete coursework.

6.
Students apply for a WE notation through the Academic Appeals office in Student Services.

7.
The purpose of an AE grade is to provide a compassionate pass for incomplete courses on medical or compassionate grounds, where course requirements have been substantially fulfilled and written evidence supports the request.
