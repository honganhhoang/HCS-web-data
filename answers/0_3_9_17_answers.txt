2.
- Psychotherapy can be used to treat depression by helping individuals understand and challenge unhelpful thoughts.
- Increasing activity levels, particularly engaging in fun activities, can also be a part of psychotherapy for depression.
- Psychotherapy can help individuals identify and challenge core beliefs that contribute to their depression.
- The workbook provides modules that guide individuals through managing their mood and challenging unhelpful thinking styles.

3.
The Vicious Cycle of Depression is a pattern in which negative thoughts and behaviors reinforce each other, leading to a worsening of depressive symptoms. This cycle can perpetuate feelings of low mood, hopelessness, and helplessness.

4.
Increasing activity levels can help with depression by improving mood, increasing energy levels, and providing a sense of achievement. It can also help to distract from negative thoughts and reduce feelings of isolation.

5.
Thoughts influence feelings in depression by causing individuals to experience negative emotions such as sadness, hopelessness, and despair. Automatic negative thoughts can worsen mood and lead to a cycle of negative thinking and feelings. Challenging unhelpful thoughts and replacing them with balanced thoughts can help improve mood and reduce symptoms of depression. Core beliefs, which are deeply held beliefs about oneself and the world, can also impact thoughts and feelings in depression.

6.
- A thought diary is a tool used to monitor unhelpful thoughts that can lead to negative emotions.
- It is used in managing depression by helping individuals examine and challenge unhelpful thoughts.
- Thought diaries can be expanded to challenge unhelpful thoughts and produce balanced thoughts.
- The diary can assist in summarizing how unhelpful thoughts have been challenged.
