3.
Phyllis (Jack) Webstad is the person whose story inspired Orange Shirt Day. Her grandmother bought her a shiny orange shirt for her first day of school, but it was taken away upon arrival at the residential school. Her story symbolizes the loss experienced by many Indigenous children and is the origin of the orange shirt tradition to honor survivors and promote awareness and reconciliation.

Orange Shirt Day originated from the story of Phyllis (Jack) Webstad, whose shiny orange shirt was taken away upon her arrival at the residential school, symbolizing the loss of innocence and the experiences of Indigenous children in residential schools. The day began as a grassroots effort in 2013 to raise awareness of residential schools and is part of the “Orange Shirt Day – Every Child Matters” campaign.
