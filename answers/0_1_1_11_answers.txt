- Critical Incidents can create a strong emotional reaction
- The reaction may interfere with an individual's ability to manage normal day-to-day activities
- Critical Incidents can have a large negative impact on the people involved or community members
- A caring and healthy community responds with empathy and compassion
- People and communities are often resilient and can manage the impact of a critical event without professional intervention
- Encouraging resilience and coping skills in individuals and communities is important
- Critical Incidents can lead to physical, emotional, cognitive/thinking, and behavioral responses
- Various types of supports, such as defusing, information sessions, and facilitated support, may be requested after a Critical Incident
