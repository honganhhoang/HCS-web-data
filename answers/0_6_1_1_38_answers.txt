The FNHA works to reform the way health care is delivered to BC First Nations through direct services and collaboration with provincial partners. It is governed by and serves BC First Nations individuals and communities.

2.
The FNHA demonstrates commitment by working with over 200 diverse First Nations communities and citizens across BC, championing culturally safe practices throughout the broader health care system. They embed cultural safety and humility into health service delivery to improve health outcomes for First Nations people. The organization fosters a respectful and inclusive work environment where respect and lateral kindness are valued, and their holistic approach to wellness, based on First Nations teachings, is incorporated into their work culture. They support Indigenous employees to become leaders in wellness through development plans, cultural orientation programs, and ongoing cultural practices such as smudging ceremonies, Monday prayers, and community events.

4.
The FNHA actively works with its health partners to embed cultural safety and humility into health service delivery. They promote culturally safe practices throughout the broader health care system and incorporate First Nations teachings into their holistic approach to wellness. Their work culture includes cultural practices such as opening the work week with a Monday morning prayer, singing the Coast Salish anthem, mindful Mondays with knowledge keepers, smudging ceremonies, and supporting cultural events like the annual Family Gathering. These initiatives help foster respect, cultural awareness, and humility within their services.

6.
FNHA offers two programs for First Nations, Inuit, and Métis peoples:

1. Raising the Canoe, FNHA’s First Nations Student Program: Provides exposure to healthcare while supporting personal and professional growth. It offers full-time term roles during January to April, May to August, and September to December.

2. Firekeepers, FNHA’s First Nations New Grad Program: Provides entry-level (non-clinical) professional developmental positions with mentorship and leadership development. Term roles start in September and May each year.

7.
The Raising the Canoe program provides First Nations, Inuit, and Métis students exposure to health care while supporting their personal and professional growth. Its goal is to match a student’s education, skills, career interests, and goals with available opportunities. The program offers full-time term roles during January to April, May to August, and September to December.

8.
The Firekeepers program is FNHA’s First Nations New Grad Program, providing entry-level (non-clinical) professional developmental positions. It aims to grow and strengthen participants' leadership skills and general understanding of the First Nations Health Authority and other major functional areas through a detailed one-year development plan including shadowing, hands-on learning, and more.

At the FNHA, employees and direct supervisors co-create goals, aligning personal strengths to department and organizational goals through our performance partnerships.
