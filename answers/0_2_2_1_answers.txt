1.
- Sexual health encompasses a wide range of factors related to sexual well-being and overall health, while sexual orientation specifically refers to who someone is attracted to sexually, emotionally, and romantically. 
- Sexual health includes care, access, and experiences of medical health relating to sexual and reproductive areas, while sexual orientation focuses on one's attractions and identity.

2.
Gender identity can impact sexual health by influencing how someone feels and identifies themselves in relation to their sexual experiences. It can also affect how they express their gender identity and how they navigate their sexual relationships and interactions. Discrimination or lack of recognition of someone's gender identity can lead to social marginalization and barriers to accessing sexual health services.

3.
Body image plays a significant role in sexual health as it impacts how someone sees and feels about their body, which can influence their sexual confidence and self-esteem. A positive body image can lead to increased sexual satisfaction and overall wellbeing.

4.
- Consent and boundaries play a crucial role in sexual health by ensuring that all interactions are consensual and respectful.
- Understanding consent and personal boundaries is essential for promoting healthy and safe sexual experiences.
- Recognizing when consent is not given, whether verbally or non-verbally, is important in maintaining respectful relationships.
- Knowing how to give and receive consent and how to respond when consent is not given helps to prevent unwanted experiences and promote positive sexual health.

5.
Relationships and intimacy affect sexual health by looking at different types of relationships, relationship dynamics, recognizing helpful and hurtful relationships, and understanding intimacy as a critical part of intimate relationships.
