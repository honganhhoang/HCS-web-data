- Anyone with a cervix, including women and TTGD individuals, age 25 to 69 should get screened regularly for cervical cancer.
- Routine screening is not recommended for individuals under the age of 25 or over the age of 69.
- Pregnant individuals should be screened with a provider-collected liquid-based cytology (LBC) sample if screening is due or overdue. Self-screening is not recommended for pregnant individuals.
- Individuals discharged from colposcopy should be recommended for colposcopy.
- Individuals with high-grade cytology results should undergo colposcopy.
- Individuals with negative cytology results should be re-screened in 3 years.
