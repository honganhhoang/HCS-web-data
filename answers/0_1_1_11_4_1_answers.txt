7.
Panic disorder is characterized by panic attacks - sudden and intense fear that may cause physical feelings such as a racing heart, shortness of breath, or nausea. Panic attacks may seem to happen for no reason at all, and people who experience panic disorder fear more attacks. In an effort to avoid triggering them, some people may change their routine.

8.
Agoraphobia is the fear of being in a situation where escape is difficult or help is hard to obtain if they experience a panic attack or other feelings of anxiety. Left untreated, more and more places are avoided and a person can become housebound.

9.
Social anxiety disorder is an exaggerated fear of social situations in which one’s behaviour can be scrutinized and evaluated by others. It is far more severe than shyness and can lead to isolation and severely impaired social relationships.

10.
- Phobias are a heightened and irrational fear of a specific object or situation that poses no real threat.
- Common phobias include fear of enclosed spaces (claustrophobia), fear of heights (acrophobia), and fear of flying (aviophobia).
- Phobias don't usually interfere with personal and work life unless the feared situation is something encountered often or that cannot be avoided.

18.
Relaxation is one of the most straightforward and effective ways to manage anxiety. Maintaining good nutrition, staying active, and sticking to a regular sleep schedule can also help relieve tension and stress, making a person less vulnerable to anxiety and its symptoms. Practicing low and slow breathing before entering stressful situations can help reduce anxiety. Frequent, short "stress breaks" throughout the day can prevent physical tension from accumulating and contributing to anxiety. Involving friends and family in the treatment plan can provide support and help manage anxiety.
