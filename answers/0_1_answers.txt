The mission of the ISC is to provide Indigenous students with programs and services that are unique, cultural, and wholistic, supporting their success and thriving with a balance of culture, tradition, and academic achievement.

2.
The ISC supports the emotional, physical, spiritual, and intellectual well-being of Indigenous students through services such as ISC Counselling, the Elders Program, and the Wellness Program.

11.
The logo represents unity, strength, and cultural identity among Indigenous peoples. It incorporates elements symbolizing Métis and Plains peoples through the circle and sweet grass, coastal crests such as the eagle and raven representing power, wisdom, and community, and features a man and woman holding a textbook to symbolize the life cycle of an Indigenous student. The design combines Indigenous cultural elements to convey unity, diversity, and the enduring spirit of Indigenous peoples within the context of post-secondary education.

12.
The ISC logo depicts a circle representing Métis and Plains peoples, the main crests of coastal peoples (eagle and raven), a man and woman holding a textbook, and elements of West Coast design. These elements symbolize unity, strength, wisdom, community, the life cycle of an Indigenous student, and the diversity of Indigenous peoples.

The ISC’s logo symbolizes the life cycle and cultural unity of Indigenous students through the sacred circle representing Indigenous peoples, with elements like the eagle and raven wings signifying power, strength, wisdom, and community. The inclusion of Métis and Plains designs, along with West Coast style elements, reflects the diversity and unity of Indigenous peoples, emphasizing their collective identity and strength.
