1.
The flu is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses that infect the nose, throat, and sometimes the lungs. It can cause mild to severe illness, and at times can lead to death.

2.
- Everyone 6 months and older
- All B.C. residents 6 months and older
- Children under 9 years 
- People 65 years and older

3.
- Under 5 years or over 65 years
- Pregnant
- Living in long-term care or assisted living
- Immunocompromised or have a chronic illness
- Children, especially younger children, are at higher risk from respiratory illnesses like flu.

4.
Enhanced vaccines are available for seniors. They are recommended for people 65 years and older.

5.
- Younger children are at higher risk for serious illness from the flu because they are more vulnerable to respiratory illnesses. 
- Younger children may not have fully developed immune systems, making them more susceptible to severe flu symptoms. 
- To prevent serious illness in younger children, it is recommended to get them immunized against the flu every year.

6.
1 dose, 2 doses

7.
- Book an appointment at a health authority clinic or pharmacy
- Online using the Get Vaccinated booking link
- Call 1-833-838-2323
- Book an appointment with your primary health care provider

8.
- Book an appointment at a health authority clinic or pharmacy
- Online using the Get Vaccinated booking link you received for your child by email or text message
- Call 1-833-838-2323
- Book an appointment with your primary health care provider, if they offer flu shots

9.
It's safe for your child to get their flu vaccine at the same time as other vaccines.

10.
4 weeks between each dose.
