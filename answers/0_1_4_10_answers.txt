1.
- The Ombudsperson at SFU is an independent, impartial, and confidential resource for students.
- They provide information and guidance on students' rights, responsibilities, university regulations, policies, and procedures.
- They may make recommendations to the university for changes to policies and procedures.
- They advocate for fairness for all students and the university community.
- They do not advocate or represent students directly but act as a confidential and impartial resource to help students learn self-advocacy skills.

2.
The Ombudsperson advocates for fairness within the university community by providing information and guidance on students' rights and responsibilities, making recommendations to the University for changes to policies and procedures, promoting discussion on institution-wide concerns, and helping students become their own advocates through self-advocacy skills.
