What are the options for accessing Indigenous Clinical Counsellors at the ISC?
- Sign up for the Wellness Program
- Get connected with one of our Indigenous Clinical Counsellors
- Attend an Elders Program Drop-in
- Connect with an ISC counsellor by booking online
- Contact Health and Counselling services at 778-782-4615, hcs_cm@sfu.ca or visit MBC 0101 to book an appointment with a doctor, nurse or counsellor

How does Tanu, the Registered Clinical Counsellor, describe her approach to working with Indigenous students?
Tanu works with students from a trauma-informed, feminist and anti-oppressive framework, taking into consideration the multiple and complex layers of Indigenous student's lives that impact their mental health and well-being. Tanu strives to create a safe, warm and empathic environment where a student can feel heard and supported. Her approach is flexible and holistic, basing her interventions on what fits best with a student's lived experience and current needs.

How can users access support through a culturally traditional approach with Elders and Knowledge Keepers?
Users may call the Intake Centre with a self-identified Elder and/or Knowledge Keeper of their choosing. If the Elder is not self-identified at the time of intake, users will be connected to one of Homewood Health's clinical experts specialized in Indigenous Culture.
